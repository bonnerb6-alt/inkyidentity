## Design Context

### Users
Creators committing to a permanent identity — tattoo artists, musicians, photographers, athletes, writers, developers. They get a QR-code tattoo and want a single scannable profile that they can edit forever without retouching the ink. Context of use: shown to people in person ("scan my arm"), or scanned from physical art/merch. The interface they edit is their dashboard; the interface other people see is their public profile (`/u/[id]`).

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Dynamic. Beautiful.** Permanent and confident — a brand built around the irreversibility of ink. The product is about *commitment*, so the visual language should feel decisive, not tentative. Editorial weight, real character, refined craft. Not "playful tech startup," not "crypto neon dapp," not generic-SaaS purple.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark, but committed-dark — not "default dark with glowing purple accents." Deep ink-black tinted slightly toward indigo, paired with a sharp warm accent (carmine/vermillion — the actual color of tattoo ink before it heals).
- **Color**: OKLCH-defined. Cold ink black (`oklch(0.13 0.012 270)`) as the substrate, warm ivory foreground, single sharp warm accent. Tinted neutrals leaning toward the substrate hue.
- **Type**: Bricolage Grotesque (display, variable — distinctive humanist-mechanical character) + Manrope (body, refined sans). High type contrast, fewer steps, generous letter-spacing on display.
- **Motion**: Restrained. One well-orchestrated entrance per surface, exponential easing. No bouncy/elastic. Decorative animation already in place (orbiting glows, spin-slow rings) should be scaled back, not multiplied.
- **Anti-references**: Linktree (too sterile), generic SaaS landing (rounded purple cards everywhere), crypto dashboards (cyan-on-dark glow), default Tailwind violet-600 marketing pages.

### Design Principles
1. **Commit, don't decorate.** Every visual choice should feel as permanent as the product. No gradient text, no decorative glow without purpose, no "AI design tells."
2. **Editorial hierarchy.** Lean on type weight and size contrast — at least a 1.25 ratio between scale steps. Headings earn their space; body earns its line-length cap.
3. **Tinted neutrals.** Every gray subtly carries the brand hue. Pure black/white never appear.
4. **One sharp accent.** The carmine warm is rare and meaningful — never used to "brighten things up." Used for true emphasis only (CTA, scanned state, single highlight word).
5. **Restraint over flourish.** When in doubt, remove. The product's gravity comes from the inking metaphor — the UI should match that quietness.
